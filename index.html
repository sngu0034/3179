Global Internet Trends in 2022

Why?
This infographic aims to show the trends such as changes in internet speed and, as well as differences in accessibility across various regions.

How?
By understanding the information surrounding internet speeds, prices, plans, and user relationships - we can gain valuable insight into the digital connectivity of the world.

Internet Usage

Taking a global perspective, we can see that many developed regions like North America , Oceania, and Europe can be seen to have a high proportion of internet users relative to their total population.

In contrast, less developed areas such as Africa are highlighted in shades of orange, being on the lower end of our scale, it represents a relatively lower proportion of internet users compared to other continents.

Internet Accessibility

In 2022, worldwide internet accessibility varied across continents.

It is important to highlight that even within a single continental region, countries may experience different levels of internet accessibility. When taking a closer look at North America, we can see a distinct example. Greenland displays the lowest number of internet users as well as having the most limited selection of internet plans, while Canada has one of the highest numbers of internet users along with the widest range of internet plans.

Another notable observation refers to the African continent, where it can be seen to display a higher frequency of occurrences and larger bubbles. This would mean that certain countries such as Malawi tend to pay a higher average price for 1GB of internet data, especially when the number of internet plans available is below 20.
